## Prerequisites

Before you begin, ensure you have the following:

<Callout kind="info">

- A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Safari)
- An email address for account verification
- Optional: Node.js `{>=18}` installed for CLI usage

</Callout>

## Create Your Account

Sign up for Александр Васильевич in under a minute.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit the Dashboard" icon="globe">
    Navigate to [https://app.example.com](https://app.example.com) and click **Sign Up**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter Your Details" icon="user">
    Provide your email, create a password, and complete the CAPTCHA.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify Email" icon="mail">
    Check your inbox and click the verification link. Your account activates immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create Your First Documentation Space

Once logged in, set up a space for your project docs.

<Steps>
  <Step title="New Space" icon="plus">
    Click **New Space** in the sidebar and enter a name like `My Project Docs`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure Settings" icon="settings">
    Select your preferred language and enable version history.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and Enter" icon="check-circle">
    Your space opens with a blank homepage ready for content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Upload Initial Project Files

Add your existing Markdown files or source code quickly. Choose your preferred method.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Web Upload" icon="upload">
    Drag and drop files directly into the space overview.

    <Image
      src="https://via.placeholder.com/800x400/3B82F6/white?text=Drag+Files+Here"
      alt="Web file upload interface"
      width="800"
      height="400"
    />
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="CLI" icon="terminal">
    Install the CLI and sync your repo.

    <CodeGroup tabs="npm,yarn" show-lines="true">
````bash
npm install -g @avdocs/cli
````
````bash
yarn global add @avdocs/cli
````
    </CodeGroup>

    Authenticate and upload:

````bash
avdocs login --api-key YOUR_API_KEY
avdocs space upload my-project --path ./docs
````
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Git Sync" icon="git-branch">
    Connect your GitHub repo for automatic syncs.

    <ParamField path="repo" param-type="string" required="true">
      Full GitHub repository URL, e.g., `https://github.com/user/my-project`.
    </ParamField>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Callout kind="tip">
  Use the CLI for large projects or CI/CD pipelines. Web upload works best for quick tests.
</Callout>

## Create Your First Document

Build a simple page to test the editor.

<Steps>
  <Step title="New Document" icon="file-plus">
    Click **New Document** and name it `introduction.mdx`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Content" icon="type">
    Paste this starter MDX:

````mdx
---
title: Introduction
description: Welcome to my project.
---

## Hello World

Your first document is live!
````
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview and Save" icon="eye">
    Switch to preview mode, then **Publish** to make it live.
  </Step>
</Steps>
